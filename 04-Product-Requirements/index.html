    Product Requirements

    Project Objective

    This project aims to investigate and develop smart plant irrigation technologies for indoor use, an improved user experience, improved product design, and product customization options for the irrigation technology. The target is to create a competitive product in the self-irrigating plant market, create sales and expand the user base more towards luxury and ease of access. We believe that adding drip irrigation continuity, soil moisture management for the water, and an interchangeable filter for solvents will increase our competitive nature with our target users.

    Stakeholders

    • Target User People with the intention of caring for and nurturing plant life, and in need of a system that can reliably distribute water to plant life. These users can include homeowners, renters, landlords, and even businesses. The device is not subject to just homes but rather to individuals or groups of people that want to ensure their plants can stay alive with less direct supervision.
    • Target purchaser Target purchasers include people who want an irrigation system with the ability to keep track and account for all variables, such as tracking moisture levels, and filtering solvents. In more specific terms: significant others of plant lovers, individuals without a green thumb, and businesses that want to ensure greenery in the work place are all key parties that we have selected as our target purchasers.
    • Individuals in Proximity Wants areas that don't have tripping hazards and are aesthetically pleasing so in order they're looking at device footprint and coloring.
    • Marketing & Sales division Looks for selling points around system functions such as moisture sensor and and solvent filter.
    • Retailers Prefer products that can be easily stored and keep tracked of. Irrigation system can be store in one container for one product.

    Use Cases

    User Story #1: Mario Mario hasn't successfully owned more than one plant at a time but his girlfriend really likes green spaces in their home. She has asked him to keep the plants alive as shes in med school with very long hours. Between keeping up with his social, work, dating life Mario is also really busy and sometimes forgets to water their plants on a regular basis. With this system Mario only needs to remember to fill up the water tank, and allow the system to regularly water his plants for him. Mario doesn't have to worry about all the details of plant care due to several of them being taken care of by the system and he gets to keep his girlfriend happy.

    User Story #2: Barry Barry is a 25-year-old student-worker who is irregularly at home between class and work schedules. Barry grew up in a house that had potted plants and loved the fresher air that came along with them. The again, he lives on his own and doesn't often remember to water them. With his new irrigation device he no longer has to worry about watering all his plants nightly or even biweekly. This frees up Barry to focus on his course work and professional development while enabling him to have healthy house plants.

    Over school breaks Barry occasionally gets the time to go out for a vacation or to visit his parents for a week. Now instead of having one of his friends come water his plants and make a mess. He can relax in style knowing that they're having fun and his plants are thriving back at home. He even sometimes gets compliments about how healthy his plants look from his neighbor Susan.

    Aspects

    The new product design will be based on existing related products with improvements based on the following requirements. The P1 - P10 is the "code" to indicate the priority of the requirement, from low to high.

    1. Hardware / Product Design

      • 1.1 The product shall be compact enough to fit on a window sill by ensuring the footprint is no larger than 150 mm x 400 mm. (P6)
      • 1.2 The product shall be easy to service and refill. (P8)
      • 1.3 The product shall avoid shatter-prone materials. (P7)
      • 1.4 Reservoir refill opening should be larger than 40 mm in diameter and requires no tools for removal. (P6)
      • 1.5 External edges rounded to radius ≥ 1.5 mm; use of impact-resistant plastics and no glass components. (P8)

    2. Software / Functionality

      • 2.1 The product shall incorporate two or more LED based alerts for user comprehension(P10)
      • 2.2 The product shall offer one or more sensor(s) focused on plant health. (P9)
      • 2.3 The product shall offer nozzles that deliver 50-150 mL/min within ±10% flow rate variation. (P8)
      • 2.4 The product shall include one LED for low water level (triggered at ≤20% tank volume), one LED for system fault; visible at 6ft in daylight. (P7)
      • 2.5 The product shall include a programmable timer with adjustable intervals (4 h – 72 h). (P7)
      • 2.6 The product shall offer a fertilizer cartridge with a capacity ≥ 50 mL with a dosing accuracy within ±10%. (P5)

    3. Customization

      • 3.1 The product shall incorporate one or more lead splits for added plants. (P10)
      • 3.2 The product shall offer a scheduling option for plant watering. (P10)
      • 3.3 The product shall offer place(s) for at least one attachment. (P10)
      • 3.4 The product has supports over 4 plant outlets with no pressure drop exceeding 15%. (P9)

    4. Manufacturing

      • 4.1 The product shall incorporate different types of connectors for tubing (EX: T - connecters, 90 Degree elbows, et. cetra). (P10)
      • 4.2 The product shall offer a way to track water level and attachment levels. (P10)
      • 4.3 The product shall provide watertight seal (IPX4) and a sensor removable without tools. (P9)

    5. Interactivity & User Experience

      • 5.1 The product shall incorporate user instructions on best spot to place attachments. (P10)
      • 5.2 The product shall offer user manual on how to connect tubing to water source and recommendations on routing. (P10)
      • 5.3 The product should offer user friendly and easily programmable controller.
      • 5.4 The product shall include a printed guide depicting, sensor placement accuracy within ±10 mm of recommended depth.
      • 5.5 The product manual includes at least 3 routing diagrams; connection steps ≤ 5; illustrations with ≥ 90% user comprehension in pilot test.
      • 5.6 The product shall offer a controller interface allows scheduling in ≤ 5 steps.

    6. Safety

      • 6.1 The product shall be able to withstand splashes of water (IPX4). (P10)
      • 6.2 The product shall have a be designed to prevent spilling (P7)
      • 6.3 The product shall have no sharp corners to endanger users. (P9)
      • 6.4 The product shall resists tipping under 10 N side force and remains upright on 10° inclined surface.

    Open Questions

    • Can we have our product be compatible with any water container size?
    • Can we create a product that blends into the home environment?
    • Can we ensure that our moisture sensors wont come out of specification.
    • Can we ensure replacement parts are included in case of hard water build up.
